# Salient Venture — Q4 2025 Sales Analysis

**Tool:** Microsoft Excel &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Domain:** E-commerce Retail &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Period:** October – December 2025

---

## Project Overview

This project is a full end-to-end sales analysis for **Salient Venture**, a fast-growing U.S. e-commerce company specialising in household and lifestyle products. The business had experienced a **consistent 20% quarter-over-quarter revenue decline across Q1–Q3 2025**, driven by poor inventory management and low customer retention.

In response, the Board of Directors commissioned a strategic overhaul — restructuring employee incentives, diversifying inventory into high-affinity categories (Gym accessories and Clothing accessories), and launching aggressive regional marketing. This project evaluates the Q4 2025 transaction data to answer the central question: **did the strategy work?**

The project was completed as part of the **10Alytics Data Analytics programme** and covers the full analytical workflow: data cleaning, pivot table construction, interactive Excel dashboard design, and a written insights presentation.

## Key Findings

- **$114.7M** total Q4 revenue from **2,000 orders** at a consistent **15.0% profit margin** — a sharp reversal from three quarters of decline (Q3 2025: $87.6M)
- **Revenue held steady week-over-week** across October–December, averaging $8–11M per week with no single-spike pattern — evidence of durable demand, not a one-off bounce
- **Texas dominated regional profit at $6.1M** (35% of the total), followed by Florida at $3.3M — both markets targeted under the aggressive regional marketing strategy outperformed the company's California home base
- **Accessories is the most profitable product category at $4.06M** — a direct result of the inventory diversification into high-affinity categories; Gym and Games (also newly added) both rank in the top half of profitability
- **Gold Bracelets** is the only product to lead in both units sold (480 units) and total profit ($783K), making it the standout single SKU of the quarter
- **Adult customers (age 26–50) drive 62% of revenue**; the Senior segment (60+) accounts for just 7.6% — a potential future growth pocket
- **Female customers generated $61.7M in revenue** vs $53.0M from male customers, a 16% gap that suggests targeted marketing opportunities
- **Top 5 customers contribute just 12% of total revenue**, indicating a healthy, non-concentrated customer base with broad-based demand

## Verdict: Did the Strategy Work?

**Yes — across all three strategic pillars.**

The performance-based incentive restructuring maintained steady weekly sales without month-end spikes. Inventory diversification into Gym and Accessories directly produced the quarter's most profitable category. And the regional marketing push generated clear results in Texas and Florida — both now outperform California, the company's headquarters state. The 2026 budget recommendation: approve it.
